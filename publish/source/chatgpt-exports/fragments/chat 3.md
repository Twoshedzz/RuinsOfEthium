# RUINS OF ETHIUM
# Session 4 – Beyond the Otyugh
### Session Summary
The party begins beside the black underground lake where they were attacked after excavating deeper into Ethium. Beyond the lake lie two possible routes:
* The Troglodyte Warrens
* The Duergar Frontier

⠀Both routes reveal more about the factions now competing for control of the ancient ruins.

# Opening Encounter – The Otyugh Lake
The black water lies still.
The smell is overwhelming.
Rotting debris and old bones line the shoreline.
The Otyugh has made its lair amongst the refuse of centuries.
### Read Aloud
The foul creature rises from the darkness, its tentacles dragging through the filth. One enormous eye fixes upon you.
A voice suddenly appears in your minds:
**HUNGRY**
**MINE**
**FOOD**
### Otyugh (Modified)
* AC 14
* HP 75

⠀Special:
* Telepathy
* Grapple
* Foul Sludge Spray (1/encounter)

⠀Treasure
Among the remains of previous explorers:
* Bronze Ethium Key
* Silver Compass (worth 25 gp)
* Healing Potion
* 60 gp mixed coinage
* Partial map of Lower Ethium

⠀The map identifies:
* Old Drainage Galleries
* Worker Access Tunnels

⠀Milestone
After defeating the Otyugh:
**The party reaches Level 3.**
As they rest, their Ethium stones pulse faintly with blue light.

# Route A – Troglodyte Warrens
This route leaves the worked halls of Ethium and enters natural caverns.
The stone becomes rough.
The air becomes humid.
The smell worsens.

# Fungus Cavern
The tunnel opens into a glowing underground forest.
Towering mushrooms stretch toward the ceiling.
Blue, purple and crimson spores drift through the darkness.
### Read Aloud
For the first time since entering Ethium, the cavern feels almost peaceful.
Giant mushrooms sway gently in unseen currents.
Tiny glowing spores drift through the air like stars.
### Myconid Survivor
Hidden amongst the fungi is a wounded Myconid.
It cannot speak.
Instead it communicates through spores and visions.
### Vision Sequence
The party experiences flashes of:
* Troglodytes dragging prisoners
* Burning fungus groves
* A strange green crystal
* Vast underground waterways
* Reeds and marshes beneath the earth

⠀The Myconids need help.

# Carrion Crawler Encounter
The true danger lurks above.
A Carrion Crawler hunts amongst the mushrooms.
It clings to the ceiling and waits for prey.
### Tactics
* Ambush from above
* Attempt to immobilise prey
* Drag victims away

⠀Reward
If the Myconids are helped:
**Spore of Resilience**
Once before the next long rest:
Reduce damage from an attack by 1d8.

# Bone Shrine
The next cavern contains a gigantic skeleton.
Not a dragon.
Not a dinosaur.
Something older.
### Ancient Marsh Leviathan
A colossal salamander-like creature from a forgotten age.
The skeleton has become a shrine.
Bones, feathers and fungus hang from it.
The Troglodytes worship it.
### The Green Stone
Inside the skull sits a corrupted Ethium Stone.
Unlike the others it glows sickly green.
Touching it reveals a vision:
### Ancient Ethium
The party sees:
* Vast underground rivers
* Elegant white towers
* Strange citizens moving through the city
* Water flowing through magnificent channels

⠀Then the vision fades.

# Prison Cave
The Troglodytes have taken captives.
The cave reeks of smoke and reptile musk.
### Prisoners
**Myconid Elder**
Wise and ancient.
Knows much about deeper Ethium.
**Sska**
Lizardfolk scout.
Calm and cautious.
**Keth**
Lizardfolk scout.
Hot-headed and eager for revenge.
### The Scaled Renewal
The Lizardfolk belong to a faction called:
**The Scaled Renewal**
Their goal is to reclaim the ancient waterways and wetlands beneath Ethium.
### Information
They reveal:
* Troglodytes were not always this aggressive.
* Everything changed after the Green Stone was discovered.
* Strange dreams now plague the tribe.
* The corruption is spreading.

⠀
# Troglodyte Warren
The largest cavern of the complex.
Smoke hangs beneath the ceiling.
Crude huts surround cooking fires.
Bones and skins hang from ropes.
The party quickly realises:
There are far too many Troglodytes to fight directly.
The statted enemies represent only those directly involved in the confrontation.
### Troglodyte Behaviour
Troglodytes do not fight like soldiers.
They:
* Ambush from darkness
* Use side tunnels
* Target isolated victims
* Retreat and reappear elsewhere

⠀They are hunters.

# Chief Grash the Deep-Touched
Leader of the tribe.
### Appearance
* One glowing green eye
* Green veins beneath scales
* Carries the Green Stone

⠀Statistics
* AC 13
* HP 35

⠀Green Stone Surge
When reduced below 18 HP:
The stone releases a pulse of corrupt energy.
Nearby Troglodytes gain advantage on attacks for one round.
### The Truth
Grash believes the Green Stone is a gift from a Deep God.
In reality it is slowly corrupting him and his tribe.

# Possible Outcomes
### Stealth Rescue
Free the prisoners and escape.
### Destroy the Stone
The corruption immediately weakens.
Many Troglodytes panic.
Some flee.
### Kill Grash
The tribe fractures into chaos.
### Full Battle
Players, Lizardfolk and Myconids versus the tribe.

# Escape Tunnel
The prisoners reveal a hidden route.
After several hours:
The air becomes humid.
Water drips constantly.
Reeds begin appearing beside underground pools.
### Read Aloud
Ahead, you hear frogs.
Then insects.
Then the distant call of something large moving through water.
The swamp begins.
End Session.

# Route B – Duergar Frontier
Unlike the Troglodyte route, this path becomes increasingly civilised.
The rough caves give way to worked stone.
Ancient Ethium architecture appears once more.

# The Frontier
The Duergar are not mining here.
Their mines lie far below.
These upper halls are:
* Guard stations
* Barracks
* Patrol routes
* Checkpoints

⠀The party has reached the edge of Duergar territory.

# Great Hall
An ancient Ethium ceremonial hall.
The Duergar have converted it into a supply station.
### Read Aloud
The chamber is vast.
Ancient statues watch from the darkness.
Crates and barrels line the walls.
Heavy chains scrape across stone.
### Sergeant Brokk
Commander of the frontier checkpoint.
* AC 16
* HP 28

⠀Not immediately hostile.
His first question:
"How did surface folk get this far?"
### Quaggoth Slaves
Two Quaggoths haul supplies through the hall.
Their collars are attached to heavy chains.
They bear whip scars.
### The Statue
One massive Ethium vessel statue still stands.
The Duergar regard it as nothing more than an old monument.

# Guard Post
A smaller checkpoint.
### Defenders
* 2 Duergar Guards
* 1 Chained Quaggoth

⠀The Quaggoth understands more than it reveals.
If freed, it may remember the party in future.

# Waystation Shrine
An old Ethium shrine converted into a patrol barracks.
### Treasure
* 35 gp
* Healing Potion
* Ethium Key Fragment

⠀The fragment matches the Bronze Ethium Key.
### Patrol Journal
Recent entries mention:
* Strange lights in deeper tunnels
* Missing patrols
* Miners refusing to descend after dark

⠀Even the Duergar fear something below.

# Barricade Junction
The final checkpoint.
Defensive walls block the passage.
Alarm bells hang overhead.
### Defenders
* Duergar Captain
* 2 Duergar Guards
* Quaggoth Brute

⠀If the alarm sounds, the Brute is released.
### Duergar Tactics
* Use Invisibility before combat
* Use Enlarge during combat
* Fall back toward the Great Gate

⠀
# The Great Gate
Do not run combat here.
This is a reveal.
### Read Aloud
The gates swing open.
Beyond stretches a cavern so vast its far side cannot be seen.
Lanterns glitter in the darkness.
Stone buildings cling to the cavern walls.
Watchtowers overlook ancient roads.
Bridges span impossible drops.
Smoke rises from hearths and forges.
Far below, deep beneath the city, blue veins of Ethium glow within the earth.
Somewhere in the darkness, you hear the distant rhythm of mining hammers.
The party has reached the first Duergar city.
The mines lie even deeper.
End Session.
