# Chapter 1
Here's the first part of the rewrite. I've deliberately slowed the pace, let the characters breathe a little more, and reduced the "one sentence paragraph" style. This should read much more like a novel while still keeping the humour and energy.

# Chapter One
# The Road to Fallcrest
The North Road wound its way through the great forests of northern Almanor like a pale ribbon cut through an endless sea of green. Towering oaks and pines crowded close to the track, their branches weaving together high overhead until only shafts of golden afternoon sunlight reached the road below. Moss covered ancient stones beside the path, birds called somewhere in the canopy, and a cool breeze carried the earthy smell of damp leaves and pine needles.
It was, Thorn Axehand decided, a very good place for an ambush.
He didn't say so aloud.
Instead, he shifted the weight of his travelling pack and rested one hand on the haft of the axe strapped across his back. Although only a few days had passed since he'd left home, travelling already felt strangely natural. There was always another bend in the road, another hill to climb and another mystery waiting somewhere ahead.
Beside him padded Loki, his faithful guard dog. The big animal walked with his nose close to the ground, sniffing every interesting scent the forest had to offer. Every now and then he disappeared into the undergrowth for a few seconds before trotting happily back to Thorn's side again.
"You know," Dave said from behind, "I always imagined adventures would involve more dragons."
Thorn glanced over his shoulder.
"We've only been travelling three days."
"I know, but dragons seem like the sort of thing that should happen quite early."
"They're probably saving themselves," Derek said.
The sturdy dwarf walked with the steady rhythm of someone who could happily march all day without complaint. His shield hung across his back, his warhammer rested comfortably on one shoulder, and despite the warm afternoon he somehow never seemed to be in a hurry.
Dave considered this.
"You think dragons plan these things?"
"I've never met one."
Before Dave could answer, a voice drifted from somewhere further behind.
"I've found treasure!"
Nobody stopped walking.
Thorn sighed.
"What is it this time, Nibbles?"
The dragonborn rogue emerged triumphantly from the bushes holding something between two fingers.
"A button."
There was a brief silence.
"It might have belonged to somebody important," Nibbles said.
"It might have belonged to somebody with a hole in their shirt," Derek replied.
Nibbles slipped the button into one of the many little pouches hanging from his belt anyway.
"You never know."
That, Thorn thought, was true enough.
The four companions had only known one another for a short time, but each was already becoming wonderfully predictable.
Dave asked endless questions about magic, monsters and almost everything else.
Derek quietly noticed things everyone else missed.
Nibbles had never seen an object he didn't want to investigate.
And Loki...
Loki simply assumed everyone they met would either become a friend or need barking at.
It wasn't a bad way to look at the world.
The road dipped gently downhill into a shallow hollow where an old oak had fallen years before. Its enormous trunk lay across part of the track, covered in moss and tiny mushrooms, leaving just enough space for travellers to squeeze around one side.
Loki stopped walking.
His ears stood upright.
The cheerful wagging of his tail disappeared.
Thorn froze immediately.
"What is it?"
The dog didn't bark.
He simply stared towards the fallen tree and gave a low, rumbling growl.
The forest fell silent.
No birds.
No insects.
Even the wind seemed to pause.
Derek noticed the change a heartbeat later.
"What have you found?"
Before Thorn could answer, there was the sharp snap of a twig somewhere ahead.
Then another.
A shrill voice shouted from behind the fallen log.
"Rich travellers!"
Half a dozen small green figures burst from the undergrowth, waving rusty swords and crude spears. Their armour looked as though it had been stolen from a dozen different people and repaired with bits of string, while their pointed ears stuck out beneath battered leather helmets.
Goblins.
The largest of them scrambled onto the fallen tree and spread his arms dramatically.
"No fighting!" he squeaked.
"We make bargain!"
Thorn didn't lower his hand from his axe.
"What sort of bargain?"
The goblin grinned, revealing a mouth full of unpleasantly sharp teeth.
"We give shiny gold."
He reached into a grubby leather pouch and threw a handful of glittering coins across the road.
They bounced and spun through the dust, flashing brilliantly in the afternoon sunlight.
Nibbles' eyes widened.
"Gold!"
Before anyone could stop him, he darted forward and scooped up the nearest coin.
He frowned.
Turned it over.
Then bent it between his fingers.
"...Hang on."
The thin metal folded like soft cheese.
"This isn't gold."
The goblins exploded into laughter.
"Fake gold!"
"Stupid traveller!"
"It works every time!"
Nibbles looked deeply offended.
"This is dishonest."
One goblin pointed a crooked finger at him.
"We goblins!"
"Yes, but..."
Nibbles searched for words.
"...still!"
The leader screeched something in Goblin and immediately every creature disappeared behind rocks, bushes and fallen logs.
Thorn's heart sank.
"They've finished talking."
The first arrow flew from the trees.
It struck the road with a sharp crack only inches from Dave's boots.
The young dragonborn wizard yelped and leapt backwards.
Another arrow whistled over Derek's head.
"Shields!" the dwarf barked.
Thorn had already drawn his axe.
"Loki!"
The guard dog needed no encouragement. With a thunderous bark he charged straight towards the fallen tree, forcing two goblins to dive out of the way.
The others scattered through the forest, shrieking with laughter as they fired more arrows from cover.
Dave ducked behind a tree, clutching his spellbook to his chest.
"I've never been shot at before!"
"Now's not the time to start collecting experiences!" Thorn shouted.
"I know!"
"I was just saying!"
Dave peered cautiously around the trunk.
One goblin had climbed onto a low branch and was trying very hard to aim another arrow.
Dave swallowed.
"I can do this."
He stretched out one hand and concentrated on the spell he had practised so many times.
Normally, the spell should have created glowing darts of magical force.
Nobody—not even Dave—had ever managed to explain why his version was different.
A potato appeared.
It shot from his fingertips with remarkable speed and struck the goblin squarely between the eyes.
The creature blinked once.
Its bow slipped from its fingers.
Then it toppled backwards out of the tree with a surprised squeak.
For a moment, the entire forest seemed to stop.
The remaining goblins stared.
Dave stared.
Even Thorn paused long enough to glance over his shoulder.
"...Did you just knock out a goblin with a potato?" Nibbles asked.
Dave blinked.
"I think I did."
The goblins looked at one another uncertainly.
One scratched his head.
Another quietly lowered his bow.
Then their leader screamed at them so loudly that the moment of confusion vanished.
Thorn smiled.
"They're distracted."
He charged.

Thorn crashed into the goblins like a falling tree.
The nearest creature barely had time to squeal before the flat of his axe sent it tumbling backwards into a patch of brambles. Another slashed wildly with a rusty sword, but Thorn caught the blow on the wooden haft of his weapon and shoved the goblin away.
Loki was already amongst them.
The great dog bounded over the fallen oak, barking furiously. Two goblins dropped their bows and fled before he even reached them. A third tried to stand its ground until Loki's growl convinced it that bravery was terribly overrated.
Behind Thorn, Derek planted himself in the middle of the road.
He raised his shield just as another volley of arrows came whistling through the trees. One struck the rim of the shield with a sharp crack before bouncing harmlessly into the undergrowth.
"Stay together!" he called.
"I'm trying!" Dave replied.
Another goblin appeared from behind a rock with a wicked grin on its face. It drew back the string of its bow.
Dave reacted without thinking.
Another potato shot through the air.
This one struck the goblin squarely in the chest. It staggered backwards in astonishment, winded more by surprise than by the impact itself, before tripping over a tree root and disappearing into a bush with a loud crash.
"I meant to do that," Dave said, sounding as surprised as anyone.
"I believe you," Derek called back, though he wasn't entirely certain he did.
Nibbles had vanished.
That worried Thorn almost as much as the goblins.
He caught sight of the rogue a moment later, slipping silently through the trees to one side of the road. While everyone else fought where the goblins expected them to be, Nibbles circled around behind.
A goblin archer, completely focused on Derek, never heard him coming.
"Hello," Nibbles whispered.
The goblin yelped, dropped its bow and spun around just in time to receive a firm shove down the grassy bank.
Nibbles grinned.
"I do enjoy sneaking."
The battle lasted only a few frantic minutes.
Without the advantage of surprise, the goblins quickly began to lose heart. Thorn's relentless advance forced them back from the road, Derek's steady defence made it impossible for them to break through, and every time one of them paused to take aim, Dave seemed to produce yet another remarkably accurate potato.
"Retreat!" shrieked the goblin leader.
"We've been potatoed!"
The remaining goblins scattered into the forest.
One disappeared into the trees to the east.
Another scrambled up a rocky slope before vanishing over the top.
A third sprinted north along a narrow woodland track, glancing over its shoulder every few seconds.
Thorn lowered his axe and watched them go.
"We've won."
Nibbles looked disappointed.
"Have we?"
Thorn frowned.
"...Yes."
"I was hoping there might be a little more treasure."
Derek bent to inspect one of the fake gold coins lying in the dust.
"It was clever," he admitted. "If we'd rushed for the coins together, those arrows would have done far more damage."
Nibbles tossed the bent coin back onto the road.
"I still think it's cheating."
"They're goblins," Thorn reminded him.
"Exactly."
"They should have fought honestly."
Nobody quite knew how to answer that.
Loki wandered back carrying a goblin boot in his mouth.
Thorn took it away before he decided to eat it.
"Good boy."
The dog looked immensely pleased with himself.
Dave retrieved his spellbook from where he had hidden it behind a tree.
"I think that went rather well."
"You nearly hid behind me," Derek pointed out.
"I did hide behind you."
"It was a very sensible shield."
As they gathered themselves, Thorn noticed something lying beside the road.
Half-hidden beneath a clump of ferns was a battered wooden shield.
Unlike the goblins' mismatched equipment, this one had once belonged to someone else.
The painted emblem was scratched and faded, but still visible.
A soaring falcon above a stone tower.
Derek recognised it immediately.
"Fallcrest."
He brushed dirt from the surface with one gloved hand.
"This belonged to one of the town guard."
The cheerful mood faded.
"They've attacked travellers before," Thorn said quietly.
"And won."
Loki was sniffing furiously at the ground again.
He followed a trail away from the road before stopping to bark impatiently.
Thorn crouched beside him.
The earth was churned with dozens of small footprints leading north through the trees.
Not just goblins.
Something heavy had been dragged the same way.
"Looks like they carried something," Derek observed.
"Or someone," Thorn replied.
Dave looked from one face to another.
"We're going after them, aren't we?"
There wasn't really any doubt.
"If they've taken prisoners," Derek said, "we can't simply leave."
Nibbles sighed theatrically.
"I suppose rescuing people is more important than searching goblins for money."
Thorn smiled.
"I'm glad you've got your priorities straight."
"I always have."
The trail wound through thick woodland where the trees grew closer together, forcing the companions to walk in single file. Broken branches marked the goblins' hurried retreat, while scraps of cloth and discarded arrows lay scattered amongst the undergrowth.
After a while, they began to notice other things.
A snapped wagon wheel.
A torn travelling cloak.
The remains of a campfire, abandoned only a day or two earlier.
Whoever had been ambushed here hadn't escaped.
The forest itself seemed to grow quieter the further they walked.
Eventually the trees opened onto a rocky hillside.
Vines hung over the grey stone, almost completely hiding a wide crack in the cliff face.
At first glance it looked like nothing more than a natural cave.
Then Thorn noticed the crude wooden lookout platform tucked amongst the rocks above the entrance.
A goblin spear leaned against it.
Smoke drifted lazily from somewhere inside.
Loki stood perfectly still.
His nose twitched.
Then, very softly, he growled.
"They're in there," Thorn said.
Derek nodded slowly.
"So is whoever they captured."
For a few moments nobody spoke.
The cave entrance yawned before them like an open mouth.
Dark.
Silent.
Waiting.
Dave swallowed.
"I liked them better when they were outside."
Nibbles drew one of his daggers and peered into the darkness.
"I wonder what they've stolen."
Thorn couldn't help smiling.
"We're rescuing prisoners first."
"Obviously."
"...Then treasure."
Derek shook his head.
"I was afraid you'd say that."
Thorn rested one hand on Loki's shoulder.
The faithful dog looked up at him, waiting for the command.
The half-orc drew a slow breath, tightened his grip on his axe, and stepped into the darkness.
One by one, the others followed.
The forest disappeared behind them.
Ahead lay only shadows... and the goblins' lair.
The trail climbed steadily through the trees before ending beneath a rocky hillside. Vines draped across a wide crack in the cliff face, almost hiding the entrance to a cave. At first glance it looked natural, but the rough wooden lookout platform built above it told a different story.
A single goblin sat on the platform with a shortbow across its knees.
It looked spectacularly bored.
The little creature yawned, scratched behind one pointed ear and lazily kicked a pebble down the slope. Every now and then it glanced into the darkness behind it rather than out into the forest, as though whatever was inside the cave annoyed it far more than anything outside.
Suddenly an angry voice echoed from somewhere below.
"Oi! Lookout!"
The goblin sighed dramatically.
"What?"
"Are you actually watching?"
"I *am* watching."
"You're useless!"
The lookout muttered something under its breath that nobody could quite make out, though it almost certainly wasn't polite.
Another goblin shouted from deeper underground.
"Ratbag's nicked my dinner again!"
"Oh, for..." the lookout groaned.
It stood up, slung its bow over one shoulder and climbed down from the platform.
"You lot sort it out yourselves," it grumbled. "I'm not chasing that stupid rat again."
Without giving the forest so much as a glance, the goblin disappeared into the cave.
The companions stared at the now unguarded entrance.
Finally Nibbles broke the silence.
"I don't think they like each other."
"I don't think goblins like anyone," Derek replied.
Thorn rested a hand on the handle of his axe.
"Come on."
Loki trotted confidently into the darkness.
Thorn followed close behind.
Derek adjusted his shield and disappeared after them.
Nibbles slipped inside with surprising speed, already scanning every shadow for hidden doors and treasure.
Dave remained where he was.
He looked at the cave.
Then back at the forest.
"I'll just... keep watch out here."
No one answered.
The others had already vanished into the darkness.
The forest suddenly felt much quieter than it had a moment before.
A branch creaked somewhere high above.
A crow called in the distance.
Dave looked nervously over his shoulder.
"...Actually..."
He hurried after the others.
"Wait for me!"
His voice echoed down the tunnel as the daylight disappeared behind him.

The cave was cooler than the forest outside.
Water dripped steadily from the ceiling, and the floor sloped gently down through twisting passages lit by smoky torches wedged into cracks in the walls. Shadows danced across the stone as the little flames flickered.
Loki slowed to a cautious walk.
His nose twitched constantly as he sorted through dozens of unfamiliar scents.
The passage soon divided into three separate tunnels.
Thorn crouched to examine the floor.
"There are tracks everywhere."
Goblin footprints covered the dust, heading in every direction.
"They've scattered," Derek observed.
"Which means they want us to do the same."
Somewhere deeper underground came the unmistakable sound of someone crying.
It was faint...
...but definitely human.
A heartbeat later another goblin yelled something unintelligible, followed by loud arguing.
"We stay together," Derek said firmly.
Everyone nodded.
Then they all looked at the tunnels again.
"We're never going to find whoever they're holding if we search one passage at a time," Thorn said quietly.
Derek didn't like it.
It showed on his face.
"I know."
For several long seconds nobody spoke.
Finally the dwarf sighed.
"I still think this is a terrible idea."
"So do I," Thorn admitted.
"But I think it's the only one."
Within moments the companions had divided up.
Thorn and Loki followed the freshest tracks.
Derek headed towards the shouting voices.
Dave chose the tunnel where thin wisps of smoke drifted along the ceiling.
Nibbles disappeared into the darkest passage he could find.
"Treasure likes dark places," he explained.
Nobody knew whether to argue.

Thorn and Loki soon entered a rough chamber piled high with stolen supplies.
Broken crates, sacks of grain and travelling packs lay scattered across the floor.
A lone goblin was struggling to drag an enormous sack towards another passage.
It looked up.
Saw Thorn.
Saw Loki.
Then immediately raised both hands.
"I surrender."
Thorn blinked.
"That was quick."
"You very big."
The goblin pointed nervously at Loki.
"Dog very bitey."
Loki wagged his tail.
The goblin looked unconvinced.
"Where's your chief?"
The goblin pointed confidently towards one tunnel.
Then another.
Then a third.
Loki gave a low warning growl.
The goblin swallowed hard.
"...First tunnel."
"Thank you."
"Can I go now?"
Thorn nodded.
The goblin ran so quickly it nearly tripped over its own feet.

Elsewhere, Derek discovered two goblins arguing over a battered steel helmet.
"It fits me."
"It doesn't."
"It definitely does."
"You can't even see!"
"I don't need to."
Derek folded his arms.
"If you've finished..."
Both goblins froze.
"...I'd quite like you to leave."
Instead, one threw the helmet.
It bounced harmlessly off Derek's shield.
A few seconds later both goblins were fleeing in opposite directions, one still wearing the helmet over its eyes.
"I was trying to be reasonable," Derek muttered.

Dave's tunnel led him to a tiny cooking fire.
A goblin sat beside it roasting something on a stick.
The two stared at one another.
"Hello," Dave said politely.
The goblin blinked.
"...Hello?"
Neither moved.
Then the goblin noticed Dave beginning the familiar movements of a spell.
Its shoulders slumped.
"Oh."
"...Potato."
"I'm afraid so."
The potato struck it squarely on the forehead.
The goblin toppled sideways into the ashes.
Dave smiled.
"I really am getting better."

Nibbles found treasure.
Or so he thought.
A locked chest sat beneath a shelf.
He rubbed his hands together.
"Now we're talking."
A few careful twists of his lockpicks later, the lid sprang open.
Inside were three bent buttons.
A dead beetle.
Another handful of fake gold.
Nibbles stared into the box.
"Goblins," he sighed.
"...have absolutely no standards."
Then he heard the crying again.
This time it was close.
He shut the lid immediately.
Treasure could wait.

The four companions reached the central chamber within moments of one another.
It was easily the largest cave they had seen so far.
Stolen goods filled every corner.
Crates were stacked against the walls.
A rough wooden cage stood near the back.
Inside sat a young woman with dark hair tied untidily behind her head.
She looked dusty, tired...
...and thoroughly annoyed.
When she saw the companions she stood immediately.
"Oh, thank goodness."
She smiled with obvious relief.
"You're definitely not goblins."
"We get that a lot," Dave said.
"My name's Mara."
"I'm Thorn."
He crossed to the cage.
"We'll have you out in a moment."
"I was beginning to wonder if anyone would ever come."
Before anyone could say another word an earsplitting shriek echoed through the chamber.
"My prisoner!"
"My cave!"
"My rat!"
The goblin chief burst from behind a stack of crates.
He wore what looked suspiciously like an old theatre curtain as a cloak and carried a rusty sword almost as long as he was tall.
Tucked beneath one arm was a large grey rat.
The rat twitched its whiskers.
Loki noticed.
The dog froze.
The goblin chief noticed Loki noticing.
"...No."
Ratbag wriggled.
The chief hugged it tighter.
"Stay."
Ratbag had other ideas.
With one determined squirm it escaped, landed neatly on the floor and sprinted across the cave.
Loki exploded after it.
The goblin chief let out a howl of despair.
"Ratbag!"
The chase tore through the chamber.
Ratbag darted beneath tables, around barrels and between piles of stolen supplies.
Loki bounded joyfully after it, scattering goblins in every direction.
The chief forgot about everything else and raced after them.
Unfortunately, Thorn stepped into his path.
The goblin swung wildly.
Thorn blocked the blow and pushed him backwards.
Around them chaos erupted.
Derek smashed the cage lock apart with a single blow of his hammer.
Mara stepped free, rubbing her wrists.
"Took your time," she said with a grin.
Dave's latest potato flattened another goblin.
Nibbles appeared behind the chief just long enough to jab him smartly in the backside before vanishing again.
The goblin squealed so loudly that dust drifted from the ceiling.
Meanwhile Ratbag had reached a dead end.
Loki cornered the unfortunate creature beside a cracked clay pot.
There was one final squeak.
Then silence.
The chief stared in horror.
"...Ratbag?"
His shoulders slumped.
It was enough.
Thorn swept the rusty sword from his hand before knocking him clean off his feet.
The remaining goblins looked at one another.
Then ran.
Within moments the cave fell silent.
Loki trotted proudly back to Thorn's side, looking immensely pleased with himself.
"You've no idea what you've done," Nibbles told the dog solemnly.
Loki wagged his tail.

Mara collected a battered leather satchel from beneath one of the stolen cloaks and hugged it tightly.
"I thought they'd lost this."
"What's inside?" Thorn asked.
She hesitated.
"Something important."
She opened the flap just enough for them to glimpse an ancient parchment covered in faded lines and strange blue symbols.
"I was carrying it to Fallcrest."
"To the wizard Nimozaran."
"The famous one?" Dave asked.
"The very same."
She fastened the satchel closed again.
"It has to reach him."
Thorn nodded.
"Then we'll make sure it does."
Mara studied the four companions for a moment before smiling.
"I think..."
"...I'd rather travel with you than without you."
Leaving the cave took much longer than entering it, mostly because Nibbles insisted on searching every crate, every sack and every suspicious-looking corner.
He found a handful of genuine coins.
A rather nice silver spoon.
And, thankfully, no more fake gold.
Outside, the evening sun painted the tops of the trees with gold.
Mara took a deep breath of fresh air.
"When we reach Fallcrest," she said, "meet me at the Blue Moon Alehouse."
"I owe you all a proper reward."
Dave brightened immediately.
"Does that include food?"
She laughed.
"It certainly does."
The companions set off down the North Road together.
Behind them, the goblin cave was finally quiet.
Ahead lay Fallcrest.
And waiting there, though none of them yet knew it, was the first whisper of a mystery that would change all of their lives forever.
