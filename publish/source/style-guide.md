# Style reference — Chronicles of Ethium

**This is the canonical voice** for all new chapters and refinements.

Published chapters 1–2 follow the **mixed rhythm** described below. Chapter 1 was rewritten first; Chapter 2 was updated to match.

When asking Cursor to write: *"Follow `publish/source/style-guide.md` exactly."*

---

## In one paragraph

**Third person, past tense**, close to the party. A **Fighting Fantasy novel for twelve-year-olds**: clear, vivid, warm, never graphic. The narrator carries more of the story than the characters do — **prose first, dialogue second**. Rhythm **changes with the scene**: faster and tighter in a fight or chase; slower and fuller when travelling, describing a place, or explaining something important. Humour comes from what happened at the table, played straight — not from the narrator commenting on it.

---

## Rhythm: mix it up

**Do not** use one-sentence paragraphs throughout. **Do** vary length to suit what is happening.

| Scene type | Rhythm |
|------------|--------|
| **Travel, arrival, quiet moments** | Longer paragraphs; scenery and thought; dialogue occasional |
| **Comedy** | Can sharpen — but not every joke needs a ping-pong exchange |
| **Fight or chase** | Shorter sentences and paragraphs; still readable, not machine-gun staccato |
| **Revelation / lore** (e.g. Tobbs on Ethium) | Slower; room for monologue; the room can quiet around one voice |
| **Chapter end** | A single strong closing image or line — not a sermon |

### Good — travel (slower, novel-like)

> The North Road wound its way through the great forests of northern Almanor like a pale ribbon cut through an endless sea of green. Towering oaks and pines crowded close to the track, their branches weaving together high overhead until only shafts of golden afternoon sunlight reached the road below.

### Good — tension (quicker, but not fragmentary)

> Loki stopped walking. His ears stood upright and the cheerful wagging of his tail disappeared. Thorn froze immediately. The forest fell silent — no birds, no insects, even the wind seemed to pause.

### Good — lore (monologue allowed)

> Tobbs traced one finger gently over the centre of the map. "This is Ethium," he said. "An ancient city — long abandoned, long looted. Or so everyone thought." He paused, and the tavern seemed to lean in. "My master believes the stones are awakening. Their magic has been quiet for generations, but recently…" He glanced towards the window. "Recently, things have begun to happen. Lights in old ruins. Animals acting strangely. People dreaming of places they have never seen."

### Avoid

- Every paragraph one sentence
- Dialogue-only scenes where narration would carry it better
- Every exchange reduced to "Probably." / "Probably."
- Purple prose or modern fantasy clichés (*"a chaotic melee ensued"*)

---

## Dialogue: less, and not always snappy

- **Default to narration.** Describe what characters do, notice, and decide. Use dialogue when it adds voice, humour, or information that prose cannot.
- **Not every moment needs a line.** A fight can be mostly narration with a few sharp shouts. A journey can go several paragraphs without speech.
- **When characters do speak**, lines can be **longer than one sentence** — especially Tobbs, Mara, Murgaddin explaining things.
- **New speaker = new paragraph** — still applies.
- **Simple tags** — `said Thorn`, `asked Dave`; no adverb piles.
- **No transcript feel** — we are reading a novel, not a play script.

### Character voices (when they speak)

| Character | Voice |
|-----------|--------|
| **Thorn** | Direct, brave, practical; not every reply is three words |
| **Derek** | Calm, dry, sensible; may say more when explaining |
| **Dave** | Young, earnest; wonder and worry |
| **Nibbles** | Cheeky, greedy; but not only quips |
| **Loki** | Actions and barks — no speech |
| **Tobbs** | Cheerful patter that can open into **longer, worried explanation**; good for lore monologue |
| **Mara** | Warm, capable |
| **Murgaddin** | Veteran, dry, measured |

## Exposition and lore

Moments like **Tobbs describing Ethium**, the stones, or the Gang of Four can **slow down**:

- The narrator sets the mood (tavern quietens, map on the table)
- One character may speak at length, with pauses and reactions from others
- Weave in **physical detail** — finger on the map, Tobbs swallowing, Mara folding her arms
- **Do not** break lore into ten tiny quips unless the table actually played it that way
- After the monologue, **return to mixed rhythm** — action or a single sharp line

---

## Humour

- Keep table humour: potatoes, poison shopping, Ratbag, tavern brawl, Loki and sausages
- **Understatement** still works, but does not require ultra-short paragraphs
- **Don't explain the joke** — play it straight in-world
- A funny line can sit inside a longer paragraph

---

## Action and combat

- Clear geography — who is where
- Pace picks up, but **not** only three-word sentences
- Competent and silly together; non-lethal (knockouts, soot, embarrassment)
- Preserve named table moments: Ratbag, "My pie!", mashed potato on cloak
- Summarise confusion where needed: *The fight was short, loud and extremely confusing* is fine

---

## Description

- Sensory and concrete: smell of ale, moss on stone, faded ink, bells at dusk
- Fighting Fantasy flavour: old maps, ruined walls, blue moon sign
- **British English** where natural: *recognised*, *amongst*, *honour*
- Let characters and places **breathe** — especially Fallcrest arrivals, Ethium approach, manor cellars

---

## Faithfulness and audience

- **Ages 12+** — read aloud or alone; no graphic violence
- **Must keep** what happened at the table: outcomes, jokes, NPC names
- **May add** scenery, interior thought, smoother transitions
- **Must not** invent major plot or change results
- **Willow** not in prose until she has played

---

## Chapter shape

1. Frontmatter in `publish/chapters/`
2. **Opening** — place and mood; can be a full paragraph or two
3. **Middle** — scenes with rhythm that fits each beat
4. **Close** — quiet hook (mystery, threat, distance)

**Length:** roughly 3,000–5,500 words per chapter; long sessions may split into two.

---

## Sounds right ✓ vs sounds wrong ✗

| ✓ Target voice | ✗ Avoid |
|----------------|---------|
| Mixed paragraph length; pace follows scene | Uniform one-sentence paragraphs |
| Narration carries the journey | Dialogue for every small beat |
| Tobbs explains Ethium in a flowing passage | Ethium lore as rapid Q&A ping-pong |
| "Dave blinked. 'That worked.'" — fine in a fight beat | Entire chapter written that way |
| Faithful, warm, FF adventure | Generic epic fantasy padding |

---

## Checklist before publishing

- [ ] Rhythm varies — slow where it should be slow, quick where it should be quick
- [ ] Dialogue used sparingly; lines can be longer when needed
- [ ] Lore/revelation scenes have room to breathe
- [ ] Table jokes and outcomes preserved
- [ ] No graphic violence; British spellings consistent
- [ ] Opens with atmosphere; closes with hook
- [ ] Characters sound like themselves when they speak
- [ ] Willow not included unless she has played
