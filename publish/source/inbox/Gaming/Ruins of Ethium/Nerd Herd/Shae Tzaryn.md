Abandoned on the streets of Waterdeep at a young age, I never knew my parents. Shunned from normal society I would have starved if it were not for my quick fingers and even quicker feet. And it was those two skills that got me through the door of the guild.

I honed my skills as a novice rogue under the tutelage of some of the greatest thieves in the city. All was coin and beauties until one mission into the Underdark left me haunted and alone.

I was using the tunnels to move between the basements of the rich when I spied a door ajar, one I had not seen before. In the room beyond were many mirrors, some cracked, some startingly perfect. In the middle of which was a chest, and a figure hunched over trying to pick the lock. I didn't recognise the old rogue, but I saw no reason why the chest wasn't mine. So I crept in to deliver the coup-de-grace, but it was another of the mirrors. Before I knew it the rogue was behind me about to deliver the killing blow, but I was too quick for him, I spun and drove my poison dagger into his heart.

As he crumpled to the floor I set out to rescue his equipment, and that's when I saw his face, it was my face, but older, much older.

I staggered away and looked up, and there looking out of a mirror, horror on their face, was another me, but not quite as old as the fellow I'd killed.

I turned and ran, and once through the door I looked back, but it was naught but a bare wall.

I don't go into the Underdark, even to this day, and I tend to stay away from folk. It is a terrible curse to know that one day you will be killed by yourself.
