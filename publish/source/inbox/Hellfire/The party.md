## Dave the Wizard — Background**

The young dragonborn was born among a proud clan that valued strength, battle, and the power of the dragon’s breath. But while others trained with claw and blade, he was drawn instead to runes, stories, and the quiet patterns of magic. His scales shimmered faintly with copper light when he cast even the smallest spell.

The elders noticed.

Rather than shaming him, they gave him a trial — **not of combat, but of wisdom**. He was entrusted with an old spellbook and a simple mission:

> _“Go into the wider world. Learn what magic truly is. Return when you understand your power.”_

One passage in his spellbook speaks of **ancient knowledge hidden near great falls and old crossroads**, where magic gathers and history leaves echoes. Those words have led him toward **Fallcrest**, a town built on ruins, water, and forgotten stories.

Now he travels with new companions, eager to learn, to prove that dragonborn can be scholars as well as warriors, and to discover what kind of wizard — and dragonborn — he will become.

## Optional flavour hooks you can use later as DM

- His elders **expect him to return one day** — success or failure
    
- His spellbook may contain **missing pages tied to Fallcrest or the Nentir Falls**
    
- He may feel pressure to succeed, but also excitement at freedom
    
- Thorn represents strength, Willow represents magic — both things he’s trying to understand

## **Thorn Axehand — Background**

Thorn Axehand was born deep in a frozen forest far to the north, in a great cave lit by firelight and the glow of ice. His tribe, the **Axe-Brood of Stonepine**, live by hunting, forging, and guarding the ancient paths of their homeland.

From a young age, Thorn showed strength, steadiness, and a rare calm in battle. He earned his battle axe in a trial of winter, proving he could survive alone in the snow for three days and return with food for his people.

Now, Thorn has left the tribe to travel south and seek adventure. He fights not for gold, but for **honour, stories, and proof that he is worthy to be a future champion of his people.**

He carries with him:

- His trusted axe
    
- His tribe’s faint face markings
    
- And the belief that great deeds lie ahead.

## Willow Starbloom *(not in campaign yet — daughter's character)*

On the night Willow was born, a shooting star fell into the forest.
The druids believe she is tied to it — that’s why she is called **Starbloom**.

She is adventuring to:
