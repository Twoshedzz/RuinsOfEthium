# SECRET — Demon and the mountain water

**DM / author only.** Do not put this in published chapter prose until the party learns it in play.

This is an **emerging theme** — plant hints over many chapters; the boys should feel something is wrong with Ethium before they understand why.

---

## The truth (hidden)

1. **Water from the mountains** that feeds Ethium — springs, runoff, and the sources that fill pools and channels across the plateau — has **changed**.
2. A **great demon** is coming to the mountains above / around Ethium. (Not yet arrived in full; the approach is what matters.)
3. **Residual magic** carried on that changed water has **stirred the Ethium Stones** — waking what slept for generations, corrupting old sites (e.g. the pool wizard), and making the whole plateau restless.

The stones waking, the Gang of Four’s interest, goblins on wolfback, the dragon, and the undead below the tower are **symptoms**. The water is the thread.

## How to hint (without revealing)

- **Water:** wrong colour in a side channel, metallic taste, too warm/cold, foam, dead fish upstream, algae that shouldn’t grow on Ethium stone
- **Stones:** hum louder after rain or when near running water; Garnel notices grandmother’s notes mention “the flow” before “the waking”
- **Dreams:** water, mountains, something vast moving under stone (reuse Tobbs’s dream imagery — don’t explain yet)
- **NPCs:** old shepherds below the plateau refuse to drink from high streams; goblins avoid certain springs
- **Pool module:** whispers at the pool, dream-flashes — attribute to “old magic” until reveal

**Do not** name the demon, describe its form, or state that water is demon-tainted until the table earns the reveal.

---

## When to reveal

- [ ] Party traces pool water **upstream** (mountain paths, collapsed aqueducts, shrine at source)
- [ ] Garnel + Mala’s book (if recovered) names the old warning about “what follows the black current”
- [ ] Tobbs / Nimozaran research in Fallcrest connects stone waking to watershed omens
- [ ] Confrontation or omen when the demon’s approach becomes undeniable

Adjust timing to session play — this is long-arc lore.
